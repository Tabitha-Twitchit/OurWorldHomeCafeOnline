  • Cafe with a mission to explain: Cyberia offers chance to check your e-mail and network over coffee and croissant
    A brief explanation of what a cybercafe is, as well as a profile of Cyberia, and its founder, Eva Pascoe and her mission in creating Cyberia. Matthew Brace, Neil Curtis Qcatch, 1994
  • All About Eva
    Conversation with Eva Pascoe the founder of Cyberia, discussing the culture, changing nature and aesthetics of cyber cafes. Susie Forbes. 1996
  • Cybsercafes@Paris
    Brief profile on the author's favorite cyber cafes in Paris. Heather Stimmler, 2000
  • Girls' Night Out on the Net
    A messy article about what women want from the web and why girl’s clubs are a must. Unknown writer, 2000
  • Cybercafes @ the Crossroads
    Written at arguably the turning point of cybercafes in the west, the writer takes stock of Cyberia after approximately 6 years in operation. The article also offers a helpful list of “dot com” themed cocktails. Mark Edmonds. 2001
  • Ten years of Cyberia
    An article commemorating the 10th anniversary of Cyberia. Nic Fleming, 2004
  • Yahoo! Mail unveils the world`s best places to surf the net; Wednesday, 1st September 2004 marks ten years of internet cafes
    A press release publicizing results for Yahoo!'s world's best cyber cafe contest. Nominations were submitted openly and judged by a panel of travel and web experts. M2 Presswire, 2004
  • Internet Café Hobo Archives
    A running series of blog posts by a BBC writer in a sort of "Around the World in 80 Days" by internet cafe format (Though focusing primarily on cafes in Africa). The blog compliments a podcast available under "Other Resources" below. The landing page contains numerous articles nested by date. Nick Baker. 2009-2010
  • The 21st Century Saloon: A Peek Inside China's Wangba, or Internet Cafes
    The author speaks to Tricia Wang, a sociologist researching internet cafes as important 3rd spaces for China's (then) newly urbanizing migrant class. Don't miss the embedded slide presentation at the end! Xiao Mina, 2011
  • Café Culture History, Part 5: The History of the Cybercafé
    A brief global history of cybercafes. Magda Romanska, 2012
  • the Weird Sketchy History of Internet Cafes
    A rundown of early cybercafe history and their links to piracy and gaming addiction. Bryan Lufkin, 2015
  • 20 years on: India’s cyber cafes disappearing as pocket internet takes over
    An exploration of the arch of internet cafes in India, and what they have done to try and stay useful. Manoj Sharma, 2016
  • Remembering The Horseshoe, Quite Possibly The Nation's First Internet Cafe
    Brief memoir with video on an early San Francisco cafe. Great comments section where people share memories. Nuala Sawyer, 2016
  • The Internet café is alive
    Covers the role of internet cafes in germany still serving immigrants and people unable to navigate bureacracy to attain a library card, as well as gamers and those seeking privacy. Sebastian Gluschak 2016
  • Apple once considered building futuristic cybercafes instead of Apple Stores
    Illustrations of a future that could have been, had Apple pursued its internet cafe idea circa 1998. By "Mix," written 2017
  • When your home is a Japanese internet cafe, but the coronavirus pandemic forces you out
    An explainer on the phenomenon of "internet cafe refugees" and COVID's impact on their already precarious living situation. Emiko Jozuka, Junko Ogura and Will Ripley, 2020
  • The rise and fall of the Cyber-café Dynasty in Nigeria
    A brief timeline style write up on cultural, financial and technological shifts that led to the decline in Cyber cafes in Nigeria. Mitchel Okorie, 2021
  • The era of neighbourhood cyber cafe is over
    Quotes from a number of Indian cyber cafe owners about their prospects and how they manage to stay afloat. unnamed Patriot bureau reporter, 2022
  • Net Nostalgia: Remembering the Glory Days of China’s Internet Cafes
    Interviews recalling the hey days of China's internet cafes, including a period after a deadly 2002 arson when many internet cafes were forced to operate underground. Roman Kierst, 2023
  • A Look into the History of Cyber Cafes: Cyber Cafe in Hong Kong
    A seemingly vintage site explaining some provenance of cybe cafes specific to Hong Kong, date unknown.
  • The Korean PC Bang: Everything To Know Before You Go!
    Brief history of PC Bangs (PC Rooms) in South Korea, and their evolution into their present form as well as utilitarian info about using them. By Asher Chase, date unknown.
